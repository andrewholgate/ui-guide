Introduction

The World Food Programme’s Online Branding Guidance was published in 2010 and is now effectively being implemented across our organization, strengthening our brand image through consistent representation.

Building on this initiative, the UI Style Guide emphasizes WFP’s commitment and potential to establishing and building our brand presence through online outreach.

This website contains a set of guidelines for WFP Communications Division, which are to be used throughout all WFP digital channels, such as websites, web applications, internal systems and other, including digital documents. These guidelines are set for fellow content creators at WFP, to help them create consistent and beautiful content for WFP's properties in an easy way.

To thrive online, it is essential to present a brand that:

  • focuses on what we do, rather than on who we are;
  • emphasizes our place in a global community by being open to healthy debate or dialogue;
  • is consistent but flexible, to adjust to the fast-changing requirements of online media.

The UI Style Guide serves as an important roadmap. However, we leave room for approved exceptions based on unanticipated needs and shifting best practices online.

Typography

Typography plays a critical role in a design. It adds personality to content, becomes its voice. When it's established poorly, elements become unreadable, or badly aligned, thus reducing the impact a given piece of content will have on the reader.

Myriad Pro type family is our primary typeface for web presentation; a nice, humanist sans-serif font, that has huge capabilities, both visual and multilingual. It can be used for both, headlines and paragraphs alike, as well as interface elements.

Typography Guidelines

Guidelines below will help you establish a good visual tone and arrange content in a readable, scannable way. These are just guidelines, so you may occassionally break the rules when needed.

Sizing Your Type

The size of your text is as important for your message, as your WFP colours are to its branding. When the type is too small or too large, it becomes illegible, thus increasing the strain on the reader's eyes, making reading uncomfortable.

To counter this problem, you should use the native browser font size, which is by default 16 pixels, as your default font-size, with a custom line-height of 1.5 of the font-size. This will make sure your text is legible and readable.
