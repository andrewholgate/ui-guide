Typography

Typography plays a critical role in a design. It adds personality to content, becomes its voice. When it's established poorly, elements become unreadable, or badly aligned, thus reducing the impact a given piece of content will have on the reader.

Myriad Pro type family is our primary typeface for web presentation; a nice, humanist sans-serif font, that has huge capabilities, both visual and multilingual. It can be used for both, headlines and paragraphs alike, as well as interface elements.

Typography Guidelines

Guidelines below will help you establish a good visual tone and arrange content in a readable, scannable way.

Sizing Your Type

The size of your text is as important for your message, as your WFP colours are to its branding. When the type is too small or too large, it becomes illegible, thus increasing the strain on the reader's eyes, making reading uncomfortable.

To counter this problem, you should use the native browser font size, which is by default 16 pixels, as your default font-size, with a custom line-height of 1.5x of the font-size. This will make your text legible and readable.

Example Font Sizes
  • 13px—an absolute minimum, used only for small interface elements, or small print (notices, image descriptions); not intended for main body text;
  • 16px—our base font size, used for majority of things, from body text, to interface elements; it's a minimum recommended size for body text;
  • 13–32px—a range of sizes covering headings, from the smallest, to the largest; these should always be displayed in bold.

Dos and Don'ts

  • Don't try to mix two typefaces within a single section, such as a paragraph or a heading. If you need to differentiate a piece of text, use stressed emphasis or strong importance.
  • Don't try to mix different sizes of text within a single section or use editing tools to apply styling or colour to the text yourself manually. Underlines are allowed only on links, but they belong to a different set of styles, separated from the above guidelines.
  • Always keep the colour contrast in mind. Your copy should be readable and easy on the eye. Try to avoid using solid white or black to style text and/or background. Our basic body text colour is #303132 and we use #f8f8f8 for the background.
  • Always keep your content plain and simple. Don't add unnecessary formatting where it's not required and leave styling the content to your desiger. This will ensure all of the content looks consistent.
